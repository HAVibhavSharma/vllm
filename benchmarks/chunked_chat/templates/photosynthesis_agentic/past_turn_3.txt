Student: How does the Calvin cycle connect to those light reactions?
Tutor: The light reactions produce ATP and NADPH, which then power the
Calvin cycle in the stroma. The Calvin cycle fixes CO2 onto ribulose-1,5-
bisphosphate via RuBisCO, reduces the resulting 3-phosphoglycerate using
NADPH, and regenerates RuBP — net producing glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate
that the plant later uses to build glucose and other sugars.
